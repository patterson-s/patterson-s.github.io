Research on Language Models

**My dissertation examines how language models can be used to analyze diplomatic discourse, with a particular focus on the UN General Assembly. Diplomatic language presents unique challenges for computational analysis due to its deliberate ambiguity and meaningful omissions. My work demonstrates new approaches for using AI to parse these subtle communication patterns while maintaining dialogue with traditional qualitative methods.**

Recent Publications and Projects

  • Domesticating Wealth Inequality (with Vincent Pouliot, 2024) - Using machine learning analysis of UN General Assembly speeches (1971-2018), we examine how countries increasingly frame economic development in managerial terms.
  • **"Placing Machine Learning into the Hermeneutic Circle: A Combined Computational-Interpretive Method for Text Analysis" (with Vincent Pouliot, 2024) - Journal of International Relations and Development**
  • **"Podcasts as Data: Mapping the Canadian Podcast Ecosystem" (with Aengus Bridgeman) - As a fellow at the Media Ecosystem Observatory, developed methods for batch downloading and transcribing podcasts using speech-to-text models.**
  • **"Large Language Models as Classification Tools: An Application for Write-in Survey Responses" (with Lee, Jaewook and Krzysztof Pelc) - Project using fine-tuned GPT models to standardize millions of job descriptions in Federal Election Commission data.**
